The Challenges of "Going Distributed"

Before choosing a distributed architecture, we must understand the "Fallacies of Distributed Computing" - things we assume are true, but aren't.

  • The network is reliable.
  • Latency is zero.
  • Bandwidth is infinite.
  • The network is secure.
  • Topology doesn't change.
  • There is one administrator.
  • Transport cost is zero.
  • The network is homogeneous.

Ignoring these leads to slow, unreliable, and insecure systems.
