Learning Objectives

Domain-Driven Design & Architectural Styles

These learning objectives are designed to guide your exploration of Domain-Driven Design concepts and Architectural Styles. Each section builds upon the previous one, helping you develop a comprehensive understanding of software architecture.

1 Architectural Fundamentals

Differentiate between architectural styles and patterns

  • Explain how styles represent high-level system structures while patterns offer reusable solutions to specific problems
  • Provide concrete examples of both styles and patterns in software architecture

Evaluate competing architectural characteristics

  • Analyze critical trade-offs between characteristics like performance, scalability, consistency, and auditability

2 Architectural Styles

Compare monolithic architectural approaches

  • Analyze layered architecture, modular monoliths, and microkernel (plugin) designs
  • Evaluate when to use closed vs. open layers in layered architectures

Apply distributed architectural approaches

  • Differentiate between microservices, service-based architecture

Implement event-driven architectural approaches

  • Compare broker vs. mediator topologies in event-driven systems
  • Explain distributed transaction patterns (like Saga) for maintaining consistency

3 Organizational and Domain Concerns

Apply Conway's Law to architectural decisions

  • Analyze how team structure influences system architecture
  • Propose organizational changes to support desired architectural outcomes

Implement domain-driven partitioning

  • Compare domain-based vs. technical partitioning strategies
  • Design systems organized around business capabilities rather than technical functions

4 Architectural Decision Making

Select appropriate architectural styles for specific contexts

  • Evaluate factors including team size, expertise, business requirements
  • Justify architectural choices based on specific advantages and risk mitigations

Plan architectural evolution

  • Evaluate when and how to transition from monolithic to distributed architectures
