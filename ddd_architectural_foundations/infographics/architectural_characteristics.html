Architectural Characteristics

What Are Architectural Characteristics?

▸

Architectural Characteristics define the capabilities and constraints of a system that are independent of the business logic (the "domain"). They specify how a system should operate, not just what it should do. They are preferred over the term "non-functional requirements" because they are critical to the system's success.

The 3 Core Criteria:

1. Nondomain Design

Specifies a design consideration not related to the core business functionality (e.g., performance, not order processing).

2. Influences Structure

Requires specific architectural decisions and structural support to be achieved (e.g., microservices for scalability).

3. Critical for Success

The system would fail or be inadequate without it. They are not nice-to-haves, but essentials.

Categories of Characteristics

▸

Operational

Performance: Response time, stress testing, and peak analysis.
Scalability: Ability to handle increased load/users.
Availability: Uptime requirements, often 24/7.
Reliability: Fail-safe needs for mission-critical systems.
Recoverability: How quickly the system must get back online after a disaster.
Continuity: Disaster recovery capability.

Structural

Maintainability: Ease of applying changes and enhancements.
Extensibility: How well new features can be added.
Configurability: How easily users can change system settings.
Portability: Ability to run on different platforms.
Localization: Support for multiple languages.

Cross-Cutting

Security: Authentication, authorization, and data protection.
Accessibility: Usable by people with disabilities.
Usability: How easy it is for users to achieve their goals.
Legal: Constraints from laws like GDPR or Sarbanes-Oxley.
Privacy: Encrypting and hiding sensitive transactions.

Cloud

On-demand Scalability: Cloud provider's ability to scale resources dynamically.
On-demand Elasticity: Flexibility as resource demands spike.
Zone-based Availability: Separating resources by computing zones for resilience.
Region-based Privacy: Storing data in specific geographic regions to comply with laws.

Architectural Characteristics at a Glance

Operational

Runtime qualities like performance and availability

Structural

Code-level qualities like maintainability

Cross-Cutting

System-wide concerns like security

Cloud

Cloud-specific capabilities and concerns

The Great Balancing Act: Trade-Offs

▸

Supporting every architectural characteristic is impossible. Each one adds complexity, cost, and often conflicts with others. Software architecture is about making informed trade-offs to find the best balance for the specific problem.

The Golden Rule of Architecture:

"Never strive for the best architecture; aim for the least worst architecture."

High Security

More encryption, validation, and checks.

↔

Lower Performance

These security measures consume CPU cycles.

Improving one characteristic often degrades another. The architect's job is to find the right compromise.
