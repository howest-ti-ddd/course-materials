DDD Lab: Context Mapping

Scenario: Campus Ticketing

The Setup: Company & Team Structure

The "Campus Ticketing" platform is a strategic project at University Digital Services (UDS). The goal is to unify a fragmented system into a single, modern platform. Several teams are involved, each with its own priorities and culture.

Core Ticketing Team ("Phoenix")

Builds the central ticketing/sales engine (Core Domain). They are an experienced, co-located agile team running on a two-week release cycle. Communication is high-bandwidth and constant.

Legacy Accounting Team

Maintains the university's central billing system from a different building. This team is siloed, risk-averse, and works on a slow, quarterly release cycle. Communication is formal and often delayed.

External Mobile App Vendor

Located in a different timezone, this vendor develops the official university app and has their own product roadmap and release schedule. All integration is handled formally via APIs and scheduled meetings.

Supporting Internal Teams

Includes the Event Management Team and the Analytics Team. They have separate backlogs and priorities but share the same overall goals. Both are downstream consumers of data.

Description

You are designing the new "Campus Ticketing" platform. It sells tickets for various university events (concerts, sports, guest lectures). Consider the different business capabilities involved based on the team structure.

There is no single fixed answer!

Focus on the reasoning behind your decisions.

Potential capabilities
  • Ticketing / Sales (browse, seat selection, buying, reserving)
  • Event Catalog (events, venues, seat maps)
  • Payments (PSP integration) and Campus Student Accounts
  • Identity & Access (students, faculty, public)
  • Reporting / Analytics (sales, attendance)
  • Email / Notifications
  • Integration with a legacy Accounting system
  • Ticket Wallet / Presentation (view tickets, QR, validation)

Materials Provided

  • Post-it notes (Bounded Context and domains)
  • Markers (4 free to choose colors):
    Color 1: Relationship lines
    Color 2: Relationship labels
    Color 3: U/D markers
    Color 4: Context names
  • Large paper

Group Task

  • Classify Subdomains: First, identify the Core, Supporting, and Generic subdomains from the scenario by assigning "potential capabilities" to each domain.
  • Define Contexts: Based on your subdomains and the team structure, identify and name 5-7 Bounded Contexts.
  • Create Post-Its: Write each Bounded Context name and its domain type (Core, Supporting, or Generic) on post-it notes using Color 4.
  • Draw Context Map: Attach the post-its on the provided paper, then draw Upstream/Downstream arrows between contexts using Color 1 for relationship lines. (Only focus on relationships with the core)
  • Label Relationships: Label each integration with a relationship pattern and integration strategy(ACL, Conformist, etc.) using Color 2. Mark upstream (U) and downstream (D) on each connection using Color 3.
  • Defend contract decision: (Accounting: ACL or Conformist?)
