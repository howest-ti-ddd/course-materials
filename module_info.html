Course Goal

Equip students (building on all S4 concepts) to use Domain-Driven Design to make sound architectural and collaborative design choices—delivered in a conference-style format.

Lesson lengths vary (≈1–3 hours) depending on topic complexity and lab depth.

By the end, students can:

  • Analyze a real domain, define Bounded Contexts, and draw Context Maps with clear relationships.
  • Compare and justify architectural styles (modular monolith, microservices, micro-kernel, pipelines, EDA) across trade-offs like scalability, elasticity, deployability, cost, and maintainability.
  • Explain when Event Sourcing fits (and when it doesn’t) and reason about its impact on design and operations.
  • Demonstrate in-depth mastery of Domain Events: discovery, naming, versioning, ordering, idempotency, and domain vs. integration events.
  • Design Aggregate interactions: transactional boundaries, invariants, consistency rules, and coordination via process managers/sagas.
  • Facilitate and participate in Event Storming (Big Picture, Process, Design) to move from sticky notes to actionable models.
  • Practice Mob Programming to turn collaborative models into clean, testable code and strengthen shared understanding.

Depth over volume: students prepare, actively engage, and consolidate understanding through practice and reflection.

Hint

At first glance, the amount of course material may seem small.

That’s intentional: we aim for depth over volume.

Plan time to truly grasp the concepts—understanding comes from practice and reflection, not passive reading.

Come to class ready to engage: ask questions

Self study

The lessons are only one part of this module. In addition, there are complementary self-study materials that will be assessed during the final exam.

Of course, you are welcome to ask questions about these topics during the lessons.

Evaluation

This evaluation of this module is based on:

  • continuous assessment through lab work (30%)
  • a final exam (70%)

Continuous assessment (30%)

The lab work is graded based on the following criteria:

  • Completeness
  • Correctness
  • Contribution

All lessons that contribute to continuous assessment are marked in the lesson plan.

However, lab work is not graded per lesson, but as a whole at the end of the module.

The weighting of continuous assessment points for each lesson may vary and will be determined by the instructor at the end of the module.

Final exam (70%)

The final exam covers all theoretical aspects of the module.

The format of the exam is a combination of multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and other quiz types to assess understanding of key concepts.

All the lessons that will be included in the final exam are marked in the lesson plan.

However, the grade of the final exam will be determined by the instructor at the end of the module.

Resit (100%)

In case of a resit, the student will retake the final exam and a new lab assignment(s) will be provided.

The ratio of continuous assessment and final exam will remain the same.

The resit counts for the entire module grade.
