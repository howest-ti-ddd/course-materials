Learning Objectives

Lesson: What is DDD? Core Principles

While reviewing the course materials, focus on these key learning objectives based on Eric Evans' talk.

The Philosophy of DDD

Articulate the core purpose of Domain-Driven Design

  • • Explain that DDD's primary goal is to manage complexity in software by focusing on the core business domain.
  • • Differentiate DDD from a purely technology-focused approach to software development.

Define the role and limitations of a "Model" in DDD

  • • Avoid the trap of trying to build one "perfect" model that captures all of reality.

Communication & Language

Master the Ubiquitous Language

  • • Analyze why this shared language is crucial for bridging the communication gap and ensuring the model is accurately reflected in code.

Practice collaborative modeling through examples

  • • Explain why modeling must be grounded in concrete examples from the domain, not abstract principles.

Strategic Design

Differentiate between Strategic and Tactical Design

  • • Explain that Strategic Design is the "big picture" of system decomposition into Bounded Contexts.
  • • Contrast this with Tactical Design, which provides the building blocks (Entities, Aggregates) used within a single model.

Identify and respect Bounded Contexts

  • • Define a Bounded Context as the explicit boundary within which a particular model and its language are consistent and valid.
  • • Analyze why identifying contexts is essential for avoiding a single, large, inconsistent model in complex systems.
