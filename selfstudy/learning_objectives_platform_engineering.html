Learning Objectives

Lesson: Platform Engineering is Domain-Driven Design

Explore how to build effective developer platforms by applying core DDD principles, based on Gregor Hohpe's insights.

The Platform as a Domain

Reframe Platform Engineering as Domain Thinking

  • • Define the platform's "domain" as the software delivery and operations lifecycle.
  • • Identify developers as the "domain experts" whose cognitive load the platform must reduce.

Analyze the purpose of a platform

  • • Explain that platforms should boost innovation through harmonization, not just enforce standards.

Designing the Platform Language

Model a domain language for developers

  • • Explain how a platform provides a meta-model of common architectures.

Identify Bounded Contexts within the platform

  • • Justify why a concept like "service" might have different meanings and models in different platform contexts.

Platform Architecture Principles

Avoid the "Pigeon-feeding" Anti-Pattern

  • • Explain the danger of creating overly simplistic abstractions ("feeding pigeons") that solve only trivial problems and prevent developers from engaging with necessary complexity.
  • • Formulate a strategy to provide meaningful abstractions that empower developers rather than just simplifying syntax.
